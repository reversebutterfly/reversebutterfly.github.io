Research direction

The common thread is simple: make methods that still work after the benchmark assumptions are removed. For video protection, that means no deployment-time ground truth, high visual fidelity, codec laundering, and transfer to stronger extractors.

01

Publisher-side protection

One-time pre-release transformations for videos that owners still want humans to view.

02

Promptable segmentation

Evaluation against SAM2-style video propagation under point, box, and mask prompts.

03

Codec robustness

Optimization and reporting that include H.264, HEVC, JPEG, and codec survival curves.

04

Experiment hygiene

Protocol-first research practice: explicit claims, failure checks, and reproducible logs.

What I optimize for

Positive method papers, clear threat models, honest baselines, and results that match the practical deployment story.

Method first

When correctness breaks, redesign the method path instead of preserving sunk cost.

No proxy downgrade

Implementation should match the claimed component, with proxy gaps made explicit.

Claim discipline

Results are used to support bounded claims, not to decorate a predetermined story.
